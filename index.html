Lessons

Lessons are live classes that are taught remotely over zoom, at a set time each week, usually after normal school hours.

During each one hour class, a small group of 2-5 students works with a teacher on a programming project. Projects are taught in a free, online program called Scratch.

Scratch

Scratch is a free coding development environment developed by the Scratch Foundation, a nonprofit organization.

It is not affiliated with CodeQuest, but it is an excellent way for beginner programmers to learn how to code.

Teachers will create a Scratch account for their students, allowing them to participate in projects.

Scratch is located at scratch.mit.edu

Costs & Payment

Each lesson is $30 per one hour lesson.

Payments can be made using zelle, venmo or paypal.

Payments can be made before or after class, but they must be made within 24 hours.

Payments made more than 24 hours after a class will incur a late fee of $10 per day.
